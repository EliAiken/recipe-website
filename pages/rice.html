White Rice with a Twist

Description

White rice in Ecuador is not the same as the commonly made rice cooker version that is ubiquitous in the US. For this recipe, we will make this dish in a rice cooker, though the traditional way is to cook it on the stovetop in a pot. This version adds a key ingredient, oil, to bring out the flavor during cooking and prevent the grains from becoming too sticky. An extra bit of oil is added to the finished product and stirred in before serving. White rice is eaten with everything in Ecuador. It truly is the Ecuadorian staple food of the costal region. Many people that I've met will even eat it three meals a day; yes, that includes at breakfast time. My father-in-law (who is native Ecuadorian) claims that "a meal without rice is not food." Ecuadorians take this food very seriously!

There really isn't much to this dish, but the extra few steps that Ecuadorians use to make their rice really helps it stand out from what you're probably accustomed to in the US. The unkept "secret" as mentioned before is to add in some oil or achiote (vegetable oil infused with annato to make it red-orange colored) before cooking as well as a good portion of salt to taste. The water covering the rice needs to taste salty, but not like the ocean salty. It is up to the preference of the cook to decide how much and the quantity of rice you make will also play a factor. Generally you need to feel the flavor of salt in the water when you taste it before beginning to cook or it won't come out quite right. All in all, don't by shy with it - Ã©chale no mas!

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup of White Rice
  • 3 Tbsp Oil or Achiote
  • Water; follow the appropriate amounts as required by your rice cooker per cup of rice
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Measure 1 cup of rice and wash well under running water until water runs clean.
  2. Add rice to rice cooker and fill with appropriate amount of water as indicated by your rice cooker's instructions. Add 1.5 tablespoons of the oil/achiote at this point.
  3. Add salt little by little and stir into the rice. Taste the water with a spoon to assure that it feels salty enough. The should not feel extremely salty.
  4. Cook according to your rice cooker's instructions for indicated amount of rice.
  5. Once done cooking, open lid, add remainder of oil/achiote, and stir into rice. Serve immediately.
